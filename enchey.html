Enchey Monastery

East Sikkim (Gangtok) • Established 1840 • Nyingma Order

History

Enchey Monastery is located approximately 3 km northeast of Gangtok's main marketplace on the Gangtok-Nathula road. Established in 1840, it belongs to the Nyingma order of Vajrayana Buddhism. The monastery was built at a site where Lama Druptob Karpo was believed to have flown and landed, hence the name "Enchey" meaning "Solitary Temple." The present structure was constructed in 1909 during the reign of Sikyong Tulku. It is one of the most visited monasteries in Sikkim due to its proximity to the capital.

Architecture & Construction

Enchey Monastery features traditional Tibetan architecture with a pagoda-style structure. The main temple has ornate wooden carvings, vibrant murals depicting Buddhist deities, and a gold-plated stupa. The construction utilized local stone and timber, with skilled craftsmen creating the elaborate roof structure and painted interiors. The monastery complex includes prayer halls, monks' quarters, and courtyards. The annual Chaam (masked dance) festival in January attracts many devotees and tourists.
