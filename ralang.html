Ralang Monastery

South Sikkim (near Ravangla) • Established 18th century • Kagyu Tradition

History

Ralang Monastery is an important Kagyu institution located near Ravangla in South Sikkim. According to local tradition, it was built to commemorate the successful pilgrimage of the fourth Chogyal of Sikkim to Tibet, where he received special blessings and relics. The monastery later expanded into Old Ralang and New Ralang complexes, together forming one of the largest monastic centres in the region. It is especially renowned for its masked dance festivals, which draw devotees from across the eastern Himalayas.

Architecture & Construction

The monastery complex includes a sprawling courtyard, multi‑storeyed monastic blocks, and a large assembly hall richly decorated with murals and thangkas. Traditional stone and timber construction is combined with modern reinforced concrete to support the large roofs required for high‑altitude snowfall. The main shrine houses large statues of Buddha, Guru Rinpoche, and the Kagyu lineage lamas. During the Pang Lhabsol and Mahakala Chaam festivals, the courtyard becomes a vibrant stage for masked dances, colourful costumes, and ritual music.
