Phensang Monastery

North Sikkim • Established 1721 • Nyingma Tradition

History

Phensang Monastery lies on a gentle slope above the North Sikkim highway, with wide views of forested hills and distant peaks. It was founded in 1721 during the reign of the third Chogyal and belongs to the Nyingma order of Tibetan Buddhism. Though damaged by fire and earthquakes at various times, the monastery has been rebuilt and continues to serve as a vibrant religious centre for surrounding villages. Its annual festival features masked dances that attract visitors from across Sikkim.

Architecture & Construction

The present structure is a long, rectangular building with a spacious assembly hall and monks’ quarters flanking a central courtyard. Whitewashed walls, red window frames, and golden roof details echo other Nyingma monasteries in the region. The interior is adorned with murals of Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and protector deities, while long rows of prayer wheels line the outer verandah. Reconstruction works after natural disasters have strengthened the building with modern materials while preserving its traditional appearance.
