Rumtek Dharma Chakra Centre

East Sikkim • Established 1966 • Karma Kagyu Tradition

History

Rumtek Monastery, also known as the Dharma Chakra Centre, is the largest monastery in Sikkim and serves as the seat in exile of the Gyalwang Karmapa of the Karma Kagyu sect. The original monastery was built in the mid-18th century by the 9th Karmapa but fell into disrepair. In 1966, the 16th Karmapa, Rangjung Rigpe Dorje, rebuilt it as a replica of the Kagyu headquarters in Tibet. It stands at approximately 5,500 feet above sea level, about 23 km from Gangtok.

Architecture & Construction

The four-storied main temple exemplifies traditional Tibetan Buddhist architecture. It features a golden roof ornament (ghanzira) with five symbolic shapes representing the Buddha families. The entrance is guarded by the Four Guardian Kings. Inside, rich Tibetan murals, intricate woodwork, and vibrant thangka paintings adorn the walls. The monastery was built using stone, wood, and traditional materials, following the exact layout of the Tsurphu Monastery in Tibet. Skilled artisans from Tibet and Sikkim contributed to its construction.
