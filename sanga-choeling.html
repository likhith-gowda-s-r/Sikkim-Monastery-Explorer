Sanga Choeling Monastery

West Sikkim (Pelling) • Established 1649–1650 • Nyingma Order

History

Sanga Choeling Monastery, meaning "Island of the Guhyakas" (secret teaching holders), was established between 1649 and 1650 by Lama Lhatsun Chempo, one of the three lamas who consecrated the first Chogyal of Sikkim. It is one of the oldest monasteries in Sikkim and is located on a ridge above Pelling. The monastery is accessible via a scenic 45-minute trek from Pelling, offering panoramic views of the Himalayas. It holds significant historical importance in the spread of Buddhism in Sikkim.

Architecture & Construction

The monastery was built using traditional Tibetan construction methods with stone foundations and wooden superstructure. Its remote hillside location required materials to be transported by foot or mule. The main prayer hall features ancient murals, thangka paintings, and statues of Guru Rinpoche and other deities. The architecture reflects early Nyingma design with a compact layout suited to the mountainous terrain. Over the centuries, the monastery has been renovated while preserving its original character and sacred artworks.
