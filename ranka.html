Ranka (Lingdum) Monastery

East Sikkim (near Gangtok) • Completed in the late 20th century • Karma Kagyu Tradition

History

Ranka, also known as Lingdum Monastery, is a relatively new but visually striking monastery located about 20 km from Gangtok. It belongs to the Karma Kagyu school of Tibetan Buddhism and serves as a large monastic training centre for young monks. Surrounded by forested hills and terraced fields, the monastery has quickly become a favourite stop for visitors due to its tranquil setting and open courtyards that welcome tourists and pilgrims alike.

Architecture & Construction

The monastery complex features a grand courtyard framed by multi‑storeyed monastic blocks and a richly decorated main temple. Its design blends traditional Tibetan motifs—multi‑tiered roofs, colourful cornices, guardian deities—with modern construction techniques in concrete and brick. Prayer wheels line the approach, while the main assembly hall houses a large statue of Buddha Shakyamuni and vivid murals depicting Buddhist cosmology. The wide open plaza makes it ideal for photography and festivals.
