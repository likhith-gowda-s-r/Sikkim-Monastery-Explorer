Tashiding Monastery

West Sikkim • Established 1651 • Nyingma Order

History

Tashiding Monastery is considered one of the most beautiful monasteries in Northeast India. Perched dramatically on a hill between the Rathong and Rangeet rivers, it was established in 1651. The name "Tashiding" means "the Devoted Central Glory." It is a key site on the Buddhist pilgrimage circuit of West Sikkim and holds immense spiritual significance. Guru Rinpoche is believed to have blessed this site, and the monastery is said to purify sins of those who behold it.

Architecture & Construction

The monastery was built on a site chosen for its natural sacred geometry—situated between two rivers with Mount Kanchenjunga in view. Traditional Tibetan Buddhist architecture employs stone masonry with whitewashed walls and ornate wooden details. The main temple features multi-tiered roofs with golden finials, intricate murals, and carved wooden columns. The Bumchu festival, held annually, draws thousands of pilgrims. Construction followed geomantic principles (sā cha) to align with spiritual energies.
