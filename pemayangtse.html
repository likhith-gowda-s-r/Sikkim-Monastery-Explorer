Pemayangtse Monastery

West Sikkim (Pelling) • Established 1647–1705 • Nyingma Order

History

Pemayangtse Monastery is one of the oldest and most important monasteries in Sikkim. Founded in 1647 by Lama Lhatsun Chempo and enlarged in 1705, it stands at 2,085 m elevation near Pelling. It belongs to the Nyingma (Ancient) order of Tibetan Buddhism and was originally built for "pure monks" (ta-tshang). The name "Pemayangtse" means "Perfect Sublime Lotus." It forms part of the Buddhist pilgrimage circuit in West Sikkim along with Dubdi and Tashiding monasteries.

Architecture & Construction

The monastery showcases distinctive wood construction with multi-tiered roofs adorned with ornate carvings, vibrant paintings, and mythical creatures. Its centerpiece is a remarkable seven-tiered wooden model of Sangthok Palri (the celestial abode of Guru Rinpoche), which is painstakingly rebuilt every three years by skilled craftsmen. The structure uses traditional Himalayan stone and timber, with intricate carved wooden doorways and richly painted Tibetan murals depicting Buddhist iconography.
